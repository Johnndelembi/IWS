What is wellness first?

First, Wellness signifies holistic well-being, encompassing an individual's mental, emotional, physical, occupational, intellectual, and spiritual dimensions. Each facet of wellness significantly impacts the overall quality of life, underscoring the importance of comprehensive consideration.

In the Absence of Well-being...

  • mental health disorders prevail
  • Impeding human emotions, cognition and behavior

thereby diminishing cognitive daily functioning. Consequently, wellness services address the complete spectrum of emotional, mental, physical, spiritual, environmental, occupational, cultural, and religious well-being, empowering individuals to lead purposeful and productive lives.

Currently in Tanzania, 70% of employees suffer from work-related stress and/or chronic health conditions. This costs companies and businesses a 35% loss in productivity. Overall, globally Mental Disorders (MD) affect millions of people. MD and psychoactive substance-related disorders are prevalent globally, leading to morbidity, disability, and premature mortality yet there are inadequate resources allocated to cater to the needs and treatment gap.
